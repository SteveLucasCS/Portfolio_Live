Interests

As you might have inferred from my diverse education and work history, I have a broad range of interests. Hockey and video games have always been my favorite pastime activities, but I spend most of my time listening to podcasts, or watching a multitude of content online.

My passion for learning is at the core of not only my interests, but also the person I have become. I grew up watching documentaries on the Science Channel, National Geographic, and The Military Channel (channels 272, 273, and 274 respectively) and rarely left that 3 channel range. As a kid and young teenager I would come home from class and listen to Morgan Freeman explain String Theory, a great way to relax while studying basic arithmatic.

As I grew up, I became more familiar with the power of Google and Wikipedia, and would Google any word I wasn't familiar with. While watching my favorite show, HOUSE M.D, this led to a lot of Wikipedia and Mayo Clinic rabbit holes, and has now left me with a completely impractical amount of random medical knowledge.

This pattern repeats itself with anything I watch or study, from history to theoretical physics, and most commonly with the broad field of computer science. I can spend hours on end listening to a podcast like Paul's Security Weekly, and Googling everything I don't understand, which ends up being the majority of each new episode.

My curiosity and constant drive to learn allow me to excel in the field of Computer Science. I have always relied heavily on self-study, even in traditional classes, which allows me to learn quickly and stay up to date with the newest technologies and tools in our field.
